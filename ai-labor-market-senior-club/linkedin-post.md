Jensen Huang told Bloomberg today that AI job-loss fears are "complete nonsense" — software engineers are increasing.

He's right about the door he's pointing at.

In our US tech corpus — 20,867 individual-contributor (IC) postings, March–May 2026 — tech has essentially two career-entry roles. Everything else is a lateral specialization you pivot into after building experience.

→ Entry doors (>10% entry+junior share): Data Analyst 21.8%, Software Engineer 14.3%, Research Scientist/Engineer ~13–14%, Frontend, Full Stack, AI Engineer 10–11%
→ Lateral specializations (<10%): Data Scientist, DevOps, Data Engineer, SRE, Backend, ML Engineer, MLE, Platform Engineer

For new grads: aim at SWE or Data Analyst first. MLE, Platform, SRE, Backend mostly want lateral candidates with prior tech experience.

Two non-obvious points. Backend Engineer at 5.8% sits with the lateral roles while Frontend at 10.8% is an entry door — the on-ramp goes frontend-first, backend later. And "AI Engineer" at 10.4% is more entry-receptive than the older "Machine Learning Engineer" title at 4.9% — but both still skew lateral.

Fortune today: labor market opening up. In our data, SW Eng core entry-share rose from 12.9% in April to 14.9% in May (chi-square p=0.012). The entry door is widening — slightly.
