A Senior ML Engineer becoming a Staff ML Engineer earns a median $59K more. That's the biggest pay jump anywhere in ML careers.

We analyzed 3,277 job postings for Data Scientists, ML Engineers, and AI Engineers across four seniority levels. The data flipped a few pieces of common advice:

→ MLOps doesn't differentiate levels. It's 10–14% of MLE postings at every seniority. Not a Staff+ signal.

→ Generative AI as a resume skill pays −$21K at fixed seniority. Prompt engineering pays −$31K. They look valuable in naive rankings only because they concentrate in higher-level AIE roles.

→ The skills that actually pay more after controlling for role and seniority are boring: JAX (+$27K), Kubernetes (+$22K), ETL (+$23K), causal inference (+$15K), computer vision (+$14K). Infrastructure and foundations, not framework-of-the-month.

→ Python drops from 60% of Senior MLE postings to 48% of Staff MLE postings. Staff engineers don't list Python because it's assumed — they list what makes the role distinctive.

→ The skills that separate Staff from Senior are role-specific. For DS it's causal inference and experimentation. For MLE it's JAX, distributed systems, and transformers. For AIE it's evaluation frameworks and agentic workflows.

The biggest career lesson from the data: optimize for the promotion over the hot-framework sprint. A mid-level MLE gets $18K for clearing Senior and another $59K for clearing Staff. No individual skill comes close.
