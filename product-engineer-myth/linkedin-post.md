"Product Engineer sits at the intersection of PM and Software Engineer."

That framing is everywhere right now. We measured it across 35,067 job postings.

The intersection is empty.

Skill-set overlap (Jaccard, top-50 skills at ≥5% prevalence):

PM ↔ SWE: 0.04 — only 2 shared skills (Agile, Data analysis)
PM ↔ PE:  0.08 — 4 shared
SWE ↔ PE: 0.43 — 22 shared

PM and SWE are nearly disjoint vocabularies. There is no fat overlap region for Product Engineer to inhabit. PE didn't land in the middle. It landed firmly inside the SWE circle, with a heavy stack tilt.

Compared to Software Engineer, PE postings ask MORE for:
• TypeScript: +36pp (58% vs 22%)
• React: +33pp
• Next.js: +16pp (19% vs 3%)
• LLM: +11pp
• Cursor: +5pp

…and LESS for: Java −17pp, C++ −12pp, Linux −6pp, Microservices −5pp.

What PE does NOT inherit from PM:
• Product management: 0%
• Product roadmap: 0%
• Product strategy: 0%
• Stakeholder management: 0%
• User research: 0%
• Cross-functional collaboration: 0%

Even softened, the PM vocabulary does not transfer.

The "Product" prefix is a stack signal — Next.js + TypeScript + LLMs + Cursor on a startup SaaS codebase — not a hybrid PM/SWE signal.

Career implication: SWE → PE is a stack switch. PM → PE is a near-total reskill.
