A press rumor says OpenAI is building a phone. The job postings say more — and the most interesting role isn't a hardware engineer.

We pulled all 746 active OpenAI postings from the Skillenai job index. **19 of them sit on a team OpenAI calls "Consumer Devices."** The team description, copied verbatim from a posting: *"end-to-end hardware and software systems… custom silicon, embedded systems, operating systems."*

The receipts:
• Operating Systems Engineer — kernel, secure boot, **battery and thermal-aware tuning**
• Camera ISP Software Engineer (image-signal-processor silicon role)
• Embedded SWE for Consumer Devices
• Sensing Engineer on the "Neosensing" team
• An "SMS Prototype Handling Specialist" inside an OpenAI **Secure Manufacturing & Stealth** team whose stated job is keeping prototypes confidential
• A Strategic Finance lead for **COGS & Supply Chain** — you only hire that role for a physical product

But here's the role that tells you the actual product vision: **two Research Engineer/Scientist roles for "Generative UI."** Their description is explicit — train models to "generate UI" for "future devices" and "help define how software works for decades to come." OpenAI is not hiring designers to lay out a fixed interface. They're hiring researchers to build a device whose **UI is generated by a model, in real time, instead of designed by a human up front.** That's a much bigger claim than "OpenAI is making a phone." It's the first serious attempt to ship a consumer device whose interface layer *is* the model.

It also helps explain the article's open question — *"will Jony Ive be forced to ship Android?"* Our data: **zero postings mention AOSP or Android Open Source.** The 10 Android engineers OpenAI is hiring are all on the ChatGPT app, not Consumer Devices. The kernel/embedded/Linux/secure-boot signature points to a **custom Linux-based OS**, which makes more sense if the OS has to host a model-rendered UI rather than the Android app sandbox.

Conspicuously *not* yet staffed in volume: acoustic, RF, antenna, mechanical engineers. Either deferred to an ODM, or the first device isn't a phone-with-its-own-modem at all.
