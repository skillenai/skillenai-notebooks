A press rumor says OpenAI is building a phone. Reading their job postings, that's only half right. The other half is more interesting.

We pulled all 746 active OpenAI postings from the Skillenai job index. 19 sit on a team OpenAI calls "Consumer Devices." The instinct is to picture an Apple-style operation — industrial designers, mechanical engineers, RF labs, anechoic chambers, antenna teams. **None of that is in the data.**

What's there instead:

→ **17 of the 19 Consumer Devices roles are software, research, or backend.** Only 2 touch hardware (Camera ISP + Embedded SWE), and even those are software-on-hardware, not hardware-design.

→ The non-Consumer-Devices hardware roles are almost all **procurement and integration**: a CoDesign Engineer for "3P" (third-party silicon), a Hardware Procurement Operations Lead, a COGS & Supply Chain finance lead, a prototype-secrecy specialist. **Zero industrial designers. Zero mechanical engineers. Zero RF/antenna engineers. Zero acoustic engineers.**

→ A dedicated **Operating Systems Engineer** is being hired to build a custom OS — kernel, secure boot, sandboxing, battery and thermal-aware tuning. Zero postings mention AOSP. So this is a custom Linux-based OS, not a forked Android.

→ The most product-defining roles on the team are **two Research Engineer/Scientists for "Generative UI"** — train models that generate the device's interface itself, dynamically, for "future devices." Today's phones ship a fixed UI laid out by humans; OpenAI is hiring researchers to ship a UI rendered by a model.

The shape of this team isn't Apple-in-2007. It's **Google-in-2007**: a software- and AI-research-heavy organization building a new operating system, with a procurement crew talking to third-party silicon partners, and no in-house industrial design at all. The bet seems to be: ship an **AI-native OS** onto a contract manufacturer's hardware, the way Android went out the door on HTC's G1. The model is the moat; the hardware is somebody else's job.

This also explains why "Will Jony Ive be forced to ship Android?" is the wrong question. Both options miss it. OpenAI isn't choosing between Apple and Android. They are quietly trying to build a third thing — and the job postings show the playbook is much closer to Mountain View 2007 than to Cupertino 2026.
