Three rankings, three different populations:

▸ By raw post volume → SEO content farms, niche industry shows, podcast RSS feeds (Peggy Smedley at 1,555 posts, Dakota Stewart, Matthew Diakonov)
▸ By per-post authority → Substack newsletters and personal sites (John Gruber, Gergely Orosz, Lenny Rachitsky, Simon Willison, Nathan Lambert, Ksenia Se, Ethan Mollick — almost all AI, ML, and SWE writers)
▸ By volume × authority → tech podcasters who manage both (Tobias Macey, Sam Charrington, Wes Bos & Scott Tolinski)

The volume-vs-authority scatter is L-shaped. Top-right is EMPTY. Zero high-volume, high-authority individual writers in our index. The closest: Jeffrey Palermo, 393 posts at authority 4.0.

Of the top 25 by per-post authority — 11 publish on Substack, 8 on personal domains, 2 on tech-tabloid Futurism. The traditional tech press doesn't surface in the BYLINE-level top 25 at all. Its authority shows up at the publication level (OpenAI, HuggingFace, AWS, The Verge) but the names of individual writers are invisible.

The implication: in 2026 the influential tech blogger is almost always a newsletter writer. The blog form has bifurcated. Substack carries the BYLINES. Traditional CMS-driven blogs carry the VOLUME.
